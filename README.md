## 〈 About 〉

<img align="right" width="340" src="https://github-readme-stats.vercel.app/api?username=martinakenna&show_icons=true&theme=tokyonight&bg_color=0d0d1a&border_color=6d28d9&title_color=7c3aed&icon_color=a78bfa&text_color=c4b5fd&hide_border=false&count_private=true" />

M.Sc. Artificial Intelligence researcher with deep expertise in **computer vision**, **machine learning**, and **real-time perception systems**. My work spans from industrial visual inspection pipelines deployed on embedded GPU hardware to stereo vision systems enabling autonomous navigation, with applied research in multimodal AI and medical image synthesis.

I build rigorous, reproducible ML systems — designing evaluation frameworks, benchmarking architectures at scale, and optimizing inference pipelines from prototype to production. My engineering philosophy centres on measurement-driven development: every model choice backed by statistical analysis, every performance claim validated under production constraints.

Currently developing a Master's thesis on generative models for synthetic medical imaging, extending a comparative evaluation study of text-conditioned chest X-ray generation. My broader interests include **sim-to-real transfer**, **6-DOF pose estimation**, and **autonomous systems perception**.

**Open To:**
- 🔬 Research internships in computer vision, generative AI, or medical imaging
- 🤝 Open-source collaboration on perception and scientific computing tools
- 🌍 International research environments and PhD opportunities

## 〈 Featured Projects 〉

<details>
<summary><b>🔬 Evaluation of Generative Models for Synthetic Medical Imaging (2026)</b></summary>

<br/>

A rigorous comparative evaluation study of four text-conditioned chest X-ray generation models on MIMIC-CXR, designed to assess not just perceptual quality but clinical semantic fidelity — the critical gap existing metrics fail to capture.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Stack** | PyTorch · MedSigLIP · DenseNet-121 · MIMIC-CXR · Slurm HPC · Python |
| **Scale** | 159 anatomical structures · 4 generative architectures · Large-scale image generation |
| **Evaluation Depth** | Image-text alignment · Downstream disease classification · Cosine similarity · Segmentation-based anatomical metrics |
| **Key Finding** | Visually realistic generations can fail to preserve clinically relevant semantic information — perceptual metrics alone are insufficient |
| **Impact** | Invited to continue as Master's Thesis at University of Bologna |
| **Repository** | [![GitHub](https://img.shields.io/badge/View_Repo-1e1b4b?style=flat-square&logo=github&logoColor=a78bfa)](https://github.com/martinakenna) |

This project introduced a multimodal evaluation framework combining image-text alignment scoring, downstream disease classification accuracy, and segmentation-derived anatomical plausibility metrics. The complete experimental workflow covered PhysioNet data acquisition, prompt engineering, large-scale HPC-based image generation, and statistical validation — establishing a principled benchmark methodology for synthetic medical image quality.

</details>

<summary><b>🤸 Sim-to-Real Transfer and Imitation Learning (2025)</b></summary>

<br/>

Systematic study of imitation learning robustness under distribution shift, implementing and benchmarking Behavior Cloning and DAgger across continuous control environments in MuJoCo.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Stack** | MuJoCo · Python · PyTorch |
| **Scale** | 50,000+ expert trajectories |
| **Environments** | HalfCheetah · Hopper |
| **Algorithms** | Behavior Cloning · DAgger |
| **Focus** | Observation noise · Distribution shift · Sim-to-real failure mode analysis |
| **Repository** | [![GitHub](https://img.shields.io/badge/View_Repo-1e1b4b?style=flat-square&logo=github&logoColor=a78bfa)](https://github.com/martinakenna) |

This project investigated the fragility of imitation learning policies under realistic deployment conditions. Evaluation protocols were designed to stress-test policies with observation noise and distributional shift, surfacing failure modes directly relevant to real-world deployment. The findings inform principled decisions about when DAgger's online correction mechanism is necessary over standard Behavior Cloning.

</details>
